Flow 07 of 15

Lab Results Review & Trending

Receiving and understanding lab results with normal range comparisons, historical trending, and provider annotations for context.

Lab Results Notifications Messaging Record Sharing Health Records Trending
1
Lab Result Notification
Notifications
▾

The patient receives a push notification that new lab results are available. The notification indicates whether results are normal, abnormal, or critical — without revealing specific values.

How This Helps
  • Severity-aware notifications set appropriate urgency expectations
  • Push delivery ensures timely awareness of results
  • HIPAA-safe preview shows status without exposing PHI on lock screens
2
Result Review with Context
Lab Results
▾

The patient opens their results and sees each value with the reference range, units, and a visual indicator (green/yellow/red). Out-of-range values are highlighted with explanatory notes.

How This Helps
  • Color-coded ranges make results instantly understandable
  • Reference ranges provide context for each value
  • Explanatory notes translate medical values into plain language
3
Historical Trending
Lab Results
▾

The patient views their cholesterol trending over the past 3 years — a line chart shows total cholesterol, LDL, HDL, and triglycerides over time. Trend direction and rate of change are noted.

How This Helps
  • Visual trending reveals patterns invisible in individual results
  • Multi-year views show the impact of lifestyle changes
  • Trend alerts catch gradual changes that individual results might miss
4
Provider Annotations
Messaging
▾

Dr. Smith has added a note: "LDL is down 15% since starting atorvastatin — great progress. Continue current dose. We'll recheck in 6 months." The patient can reply with questions.

How This Helps
  • Provider annotations add clinical context patients need
  • Reply capability reduces unnecessary office visits for questions
  • Annotation timestamps create a communication record
5
Share with Another Provider
Record Sharing
▾

The patient shares their lab results with their cardiologist through a secure sharing link. The recipient gets time-limited access to the specific results without needing a GoHealth account.

How This Helps
  • Secure sharing eliminates faxing and mailing paper results
  • Time-limited links prevent indefinite access to sensitive data
  • Cross-provider sharing improves care coordination
