Patient Sign-Up & Identity Verification

Complete onboarding journey from creating an account to verifying identity — covering email registration, MFA setup, identity proofing, and consent management.

Registration MFA Identity Verification Consent Forms Dashboard Notifications
1
Create Account
Registration
▾

A new patient visits the GoHealth portal and registers with their email address. The system validates the email format, checks for duplicate accounts, and sends a verification link.

How This Helps
  • Email verification prevents fake account creation
  • Duplicate detection avoids multiple records for the same patient
  • Welcome email sets expectations for the onboarding process
2
Email Verification
Registration
▾

The patient clicks the verification link and is redirected to complete their profile — full name, date of birth, phone number, and emergency contact. Required fields are validated in real-time.

How This Helps
  • Real-time validation catches errors before submission
  • Progressive form design reduces cognitive load
  • Emergency contact collection ensures safety in urgent situations
3
Multi-Factor Authentication Setup
MFA
▾

The patient enables MFA by scanning a QR code with their authenticator app or receiving an SMS code. The system requires at least one second factor for account security.

How This Helps
  • TOTP and SMS options give patients flexibility in security method
  • Required MFA meets HIPAA security requirements
  • Recovery codes are generated for account recovery scenarios
4
Identity Proofing
Identity Verification
▾

For access to medical records, the patient completes identity proofing — uploading a government ID and answering knowledge-based questions matched against healthcare databases.

How This Helps
  • KBA questions prevent unauthorized access to medical records
  • ID document verification adds a physical identity layer
  • Identity proofing level determines which records the patient can access
5
Consent & Terms Acceptance
Consent Forms
▾

The patient reviews and accepts the terms of use, privacy policy, and HIPAA consent forms. Digital signatures are captured and stored with timestamps for compliance.

How This Helps
  • Digital consent capture eliminates paper forms
  • Timestamped acceptance creates a complete audit trail
  • Version-tracked policies ensure patients agree to current terms
6
Profile Activation
Dashboard
▾

The account is activated and the patient is directed to their dashboard. The system triggers a welcome notification and prompts them to link their provider for record access.

How This Helps
  • Guided onboarding reduces time-to-value for new patients
  • Provider linking prompt accelerates access to health records
  • Welcome notification includes helpful getting-started tips
